# SEO Recommendations for Bear Lake Jason

This site is still early: no live MLS search, no blog, and a relatively small page set. That is okay. The best SEO strategy right now is not to create thin pages or make inflated claims; it is to build a technically clean site with genuinely useful local guidance that can later support listings, community pages, and market content.

## What was improved in this pass

1. **Technical SEO baseline**
   - Kept canonical URLs and sitemap support in place.
   - Added an index/follow robots meta tag with `max-image-preview:large` so Google can show larger preview images when eligible.
   - Added Open Graph site name and locale metadata for cleaner social sharing.
   - Expanded structured data beyond a basic agent object to include the agent, brokerage, website, page-level schema, breadcrumbs where useful, and FAQ schema where matching FAQ content is visible on the page.

2. **Useful content without overreaching**
   - Added a homepage section that explains how buyers and sellers should start researching Bear Lake property while the site is still growing.
   - Added visible FAQs for common early-stage questions.
   - Added a single `Bear Lake Communities` guide instead of creating multiple thin town pages. This is more calibrated because it gives visitors a real comparison resource without pretending the site has deep area content yet.
   - Added rental-aware FAQs that are careful about no guaranteed rental income, occupancy, resale value, or investment performance.

3. **Internal linking**
   - Added navigation to the new community guide.
   - Linked homepage users toward the community guide and buyer/seller guidance.
   - Kept calls to action focused on contacting Jason for current listings and property-specific local context.

## Recommended next SEO priorities

### 1. Add Google Business Profile work before heavy content expansion

For a local REALTOR® site, Google Business Profile work is likely more important than publishing a large blog immediately.

Specific actions:

- Confirm the business name, category, address/service area, phone number, website URL, and hours are accurate.
- Add high-quality real photos: Jason, brokerage, Bear Lake scenery, local office, and non-stock community images.
- Ask happy clients for honest reviews in a compliant, non-incentivized way.
- Post occasional updates tied to local usefulness: buyer tips, seller prep, seasonal access reminders, and community notes.
- Make sure name/address/phone formatting is consistent everywhere online.

### 2. Build one strong community hub before town-specific pages

The new `/bear-lake-communities/` page should become the hub for area comparisons. Do not create separate Garden City, Fish Haven, Laketown, and St. Charles pages until each page can be genuinely useful.

Only create a town page when it can include:

- 700+ words of original local insight.
- Photos from that area.
- Common buyer questions for that town.
- Local property types and tradeoffs.
- Clear internal links to contact, buying/selling, rental guidance, and any future listings.
- No copied MLS descriptions or generic AI filler.

Suggested future URLs:

- `/bear-lake-communities/garden-city-ut-real-estate/`
- `/bear-lake-communities/fish-haven-id-real-estate/`
- `/bear-lake-communities/laketown-ut-real-estate/`
- `/bear-lake-communities/st-charles-id-real-estate/`

### 3. Add live MLS/listing search only when it is technically and legally clean

Live listings can help SEO and conversions, but only if implementation is reliable and compliant with MLS/IDX rules.

Before launch:

- Confirm IDX/MLS rules for display, attribution, disclaimers, refresh timing, and sold/pending data.
- Make sure listing pages are crawlable if the MLS/IDX agreement allows it.
- Avoid indexing low-value search-result combinations that create duplicate or thin pages.
- Create a few curated indexable search pages, such as:
  - Bear Lake cabins for sale
  - Bear Lake building lots for sale
  - Garden City homes for sale
  - Fish Haven homes for sale
  - Bear Lake condos and townhomes for sale
- Add `noindex` to endless filtered search combinations if they do not provide unique value.

### 4. Start with practical evergreen guides, not a high-volume blog

A small number of durable, local guides is better than frequent thin posts.

Recommended first guides:

1. `Buying a Bear Lake Cabin: Access, Utilities, HOA Rules, and Seasonal Questions`
2. `Utah vs. Idaho Bear Lake Property: What Buyers Should Compare`
3. `Buying a Bear Lake Building Lot: Utilities, Slope, Water, Septic, and Builder Fit`
4. `Preparing a Bear Lake Property to Sell: Pricing, Seasonality, and Buyer Expectations`
5. `Bear Lake Short-Term Rental Due Diligence: Rules, HOA Documents, Parking, and Management`

Each guide should include:

- Jason's local perspective.
- A clear disclaimer where needed.
- Internal links to contact and related pages.
- Original photos when possible.
- A short FAQ section.
- A clear date updated if the topic changes over time.

### 5. Improve conversion tracking before spending on ads

Set up measurement before paying for traffic.

Track at minimum:

- Contact form submissions.
- Phone link clicks.
- SMS link clicks.
- Email link clicks.
- Visits to the contact page.
- Source/medium for each lead where possible.

Recommended tools:

- Google Search Console.
- Google Analytics 4 or a privacy-friendly analytics tool.
- Google Tag Manager if you want cleaner event management.
- Call tracking only if it does not create confusing NAP consistency issues for local SEO.

### 6. Add trust signals carefully

Good real estate SEO depends heavily on trust.

Useful additions:

- Real Jason headshot and local photos rather than placeholders.
- License details if available and appropriate.
- Brokerage relationship clearly visible.
- Equal Housing Opportunity statement.
- Testimonials only if permission/compliance is clear.
- No fake review markup and no unverified claims such as "#1 Bear Lake agent" unless there is documented proof.

## Google Ads: are they worth paying for?

### Short answer

Google Ads can be worth testing, but I would not spend heavily yet. The site is credible enough for a small, controlled lead-generation test, but it does not yet have live listings, deep community pages, or strong conversion tracking. Those gaps can make paid clicks less efficient.

### Recommended approach

Run Google Ads only as a measured experiment after conversion tracking is working.

Suggested test:

- Budget: start around **$500-$1,500/month** for 60-90 days.
- Campaign type: Search campaigns only at first, not Performance Max.
- Geography: tightly target Bear Lake-area intent and likely feeder markets if Jason wants them, such as Utah/Idaho audiences researching second homes.
- Keywords: focus on high-intent terms, not broad research terms.
- Landing pages: send people to the most relevant page, not always the homepage.
- Success metric: qualified conversations, not just form fills or clicks.

Potential good keyword themes:

- `bear lake realtor`
- `bear lake real estate agent`
- `garden city ut realtor`
- `bear lake cabins for sale`
- `bear lake lots for sale`
- `fish haven id real estate`
- `bear lake vacation rental property`

Keywords to avoid or tightly control:

- Very broad terms like `utah real estate`.
- Informational-only terms like `things to do in bear lake` unless there is a separate content strategy.
- Competitor names unless Jason has a specific reason and understands the risk/cost.
- Broad-match campaigns without close monitoring.

### When ads are more likely to be worth it

Ads become more attractive when:

- The site has working conversion tracking.
- There are landing pages for cabins, lots, communities, and rental-aware buyers.
- Jason can respond quickly to calls/forms.
- Lead quality is reviewed weekly.
- Negative keywords are updated often.
- The cost per qualified lead is acceptable relative to commission potential.

### When ads are probably not worth it yet

Pause or avoid ads if:

- Calls and forms are not being tracked.
- Most leads are unqualified or outside the service area.
- The site cannot answer the visitor's basic intent.
- There is no time to review search terms and add negatives.
- The campaign is optimized for clicks instead of qualified leads.

## Suggested 90-day SEO roadmap

### Days 1-30

- Verify Google Search Console and submit sitemap.
- Set up conversion tracking.
- Finish Google Business Profile optimization.
- Replace any placeholder imagery with real local photos where possible.
- Add license/compliance details if appropriate.

### Days 31-60

- Expand the Bear Lake Communities hub with more detail and original photos.
- Add one strong evergreen guide for building lots or cabins.
- Add internal links from existing pages to the new guide.
- Review Search Console queries and adjust page titles/descriptions if impressions appear for unexpected terms.

### Days 61-90

- Add a second evergreen guide.
- Decide whether IDX/listing integration is ready.
- If tracking is working, run a small Google Ads Search test.
- Review leads by source and quality, not just traffic volume.

## What not to do

- Do not create dozens of thin city/keyword pages.
- Do not use fake reviews, fake awards, or inflated performance claims.
- Do not promise rental income or investment returns.
- Do not index every possible future MLS filter combination.
- Do not pay for broad Google Ads traffic before tracking is in place.
- Do not publish generic blog posts that could apply to any real estate market.

## Bottom line

The site should pursue SEO by becoming the most useful local starting point for serious Bear Lake buyers and sellers. Right now, the best path is a strong technical foundation, careful local content, consistent Google Business Profile work, and measured paid search only after tracking and landing pages are ready.
